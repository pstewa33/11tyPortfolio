About

I’m a UX Engineer who works at the intersection of design and front-end development. My focus is building design systems, reusable components, and scalable front-end architecture that help teams ship consistent, accessible experiences.

Over the past five years, I’ve worked primarily on large public-sector platforms where usability, accessibility, and long-term maintainability are critical. Much of my work involves translating design standards into working systems—developing components, templates, and interaction patterns that can be implemented across multiple sites and teams.

I’m particularly interested in how design systems evolve over time. That includes migrating legacy frameworks, improving documentation, and creating governance models that help teams adopt systems successfully rather than working around them.

My approach is rooted in accessibility and practicality. I often build code-based prototypes to validate interaction behavior, responsive layouts, and accessibility requirements before development begins. This helps teams align earlier in the process and reduces rework later in the build phase.

Outside of project work, I enjoy exploring front-end tools, interactive design experiments, and new approaches to component-driven development.
