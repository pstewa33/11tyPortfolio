Context

Smokefree Vet NRT Redesign

Role

UX Designer

Timeline

X months

(04, 2025 - 07, 2025)

Tools

Figma, Crazy Egg

Team

UX Designer, Content Strategist

Overview

SmokefreeVET is a U.S. Department of Health and Human Services initiative that helps Veterans quit tobacco through evidence-based resources, including Nicotine Replacement Therapy (NRT).


This project focused on modernizing the NRT experience by replacing downloadable PDF fact sheets with web-based content and restructuring the NRT landing pageâ€™s information hierarchy to better support user needs and stakeholder goals.

Project Goals

  • Improve User Experience

  • Boost Search Engine Optimization

  • Prioritize Useful Content

The Problem

What challenges are users facing?

The Smokefree Veterans Quit with NRT Interactive Tool relied on outdated interactions and content that limited usability, discoverability, and content effectiveness. Our challenge was to redesign the experience to better serve Veterans, improve SEO performance, and highlight the most useful information.

Discover

Research and Discovery

Using heatmaps, click-through rates, and behavioral analytics, we evaluated user interaction patterns to uncover engagement gaps and usability challenges within the experience.

Analytics Review

Utilized Crazy Egg analytics tools to understand how users interacted with the page and to pinpoint engagement drop-offs and usability issues.

Crazy Egg Analytics (show to the right)

Key Research Findings

  • The dosage graphics had a significant amount of dead clicks and rage clicks.

  • Long Scroll - users stopped before getting to the high priority content lower on the page.

  • Fact sheets were static PDFs that would need frequent updates

Sketches & Wireframes

NRT Factsheet Wireframes

Moving from static documents to webpages allowed for interactivity, which allowed for more flexibility in design. In order to reduce cognitive load we increased whitespace and implemented content grouping via tabs. We updated the IA to allow for navigation between the various fact sheets as well.

Key Changes

  • Increase white space

  • Added user interactivity with clickable components (tabs)

  • Added cards to facilitate navigation between various fact sheets.

Reflection

Key Takeaways

Reflections and learnings from this project that will inform future work.

What I Learned

  • Early and continuous user testing is crucial - testing assumptions early saved weeks of design time

  • Quantitative data tells you what is happening, but qualitative research tells you why

  • Sometimes the simplest solution is the best - removing features can be as valuable as adding them

  • Cross-functional collaboration led to better solutions than working in isolation

Next Steps

  • Monitor analytics to validate the improvements continue over time

  • Conduct follow-up interviews to gather deeper insights

  • Expand the design system to other product areas

  • Explore additional personalization features based on user segments
